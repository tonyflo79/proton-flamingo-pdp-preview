FAQ

Questions, answered.

Will the Flamingo be banned at my next tournament?+

No. Series Three — Project Flamingo is dual-stamp certified by both UPA and USA Pickleball, the two governing bodies that set the ceiling for sanctioned tournament play — pushing the legal max. One paddle, both rule sets. It is playable at the legal ceiling of every league and tournament that uses either spec. If a future rule change moves the ceiling, we'll address it then.

How is the roughness different from a textured or coated paddle?+

The roughness is part of the carbon itself, structurally integrated during construction. It's structural — the face is rough because the carbon is rough. That's a different thing from a finish or treatment that sits on the surface. The texture is built into the aerospace grade carbon fiber, the same composite discipline used in high-stress structural applications.

Elongated or Wide-Body — which one is for me?+

Elongated has a longer hitting surface and more reach — players who play singles, want more speed, and accept a slightly tighter sweet spot tend to pick it. Wide-Body has a shorter, wider face with a larger sweet spot — players who play more doubles or want forgiveness on off-center hits tend to pick it. Same construction across both.

13mm or 15mm — what's the difference?+

13mm is faster pop. Less dwell time on the face, quicker return on counters and put-aways. 15mm is more control. More dwell time on the face, steadier on resets, drops, and dinks. Same Velocity Foam Core, same AeroGrade Construction — the difference is feel.
